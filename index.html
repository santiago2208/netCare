Certified Nursing Assistant

TASKS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

Providing basic care to patients

  • Answer patient call lights
  • Assist patients with daily living activities like bathing, dressing, and grooming
  • Serves meals to patients and assists them in eating and drinking
  • Regularly turns and repositions patients to prevent bedsores
  • Finds out the health concerns of patients and reports health information to nurses
  • Transports patients from their beds to wheelchairs and vice versa
  • Monitors vital signs of patients, including blood pressure, temperature, pulse rate, etc

Non- common:

  • Maintains the supplies inventory, cleans the surroundings, and changes bed linens, if assigned to do so
  • Administers certain medication and treatments, if qualified and authorized to do so

Licensed Practical Nurse

Licensed Practical Nurse

High-level caregiving responsibilities than CNAs.

  • Administer medication
  • vaccinations
  • Collect samples
  • Assist with wound care
  • Perform emergency Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation
  • Execute a nursing care plan under the direction of an RN (registered nurse)
  • Care for patients with ventilators
  • Monitors vital signs of patients, including blood pressure, temperature, pulse rate, etc
  • Assist with patient's activities of daily living

Medical Assistant

  • Organizes and updates health records by inputting patients’ diagnoses, treatment plans, medical history, and personal information
  • Schedules doctor’s appointments and medical tests for patients
  • Submits medical forms and insurance claim forms
  • Makes arrangements for hospital admissions
  • Performs billing and bookkeeping duties
  • Answers and makes phone calls
  • Assists doctors during patient checkups and examinations
  • Takes vital signs of patients, such as blood pressure, temperature, heart rate, weight, height, etc.
